Listening grammar

The music wants polish, but it still needs a little smoke on the glass.

The pattern is clean rather than loud: basslines with posture, chords with warmth, hooks with memory, and rhythm that moves without shouting.

Reading the pattern

The taste prefers groove over noise.

The through-line is not simply old music or dance music. It is restraint with a pulse: arrangements that leave space, rhythm sections with body, and melodies that sound smooth without becoming weightless.
